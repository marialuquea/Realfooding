Let's make a healthy grocery list!

  1. Bakery and Bread
    • Whole wheat bread
    • Whole-grain flour tortillas
  2. Meat and Seafood
    • Skinless chicken or turkey breasts
    • Ground turkey or chicken
    • Salmon, halibut, trout, mackerel, or your favourite seafood
    • Reduced-sodium lunchmeat (turkey, roast beef)
    • If you buy red meat, choose the leanest cuts -- ones with very little marbling.
  3. Pasta and Rice
    • Brown rice
    • Whole wheat or whole-grain pasta
  4. Oils, Sauces, Salad Dressings, and Condiments
    • Tomato sauce
    • Red-wine vinegar
    • Extra virgin olive oil, canola oil, nonfat cooking spray
    • Olives
  5. Cereals and Breakfast Foods
    • Whole-grain or multigrain cereals
    • Steel-cut or instant oatmeal
    • Whole-grain cereal bars
  6. Soups and Canned goods
    • Diced or whole peeled tomatoes
    • Tuna or salmon packed in water
    • Low-sodium soups and broths
    • Black, kidney, soy, or garbanzo beans; lentils, split peas
  7. Frozen Foods
    • Frozen vegetables: broccoli, spinach, peas, and carrots (no sauce)
    • rozen fruit: strawberries, raspberries, blueberries (without added sugar)
    • Whole-grain vegetable pizza
  8. Dairy (skim or soy milk), free fat cheese and eggs
  9. Dried fruit and seeds
  10. Hummus
  11. Dark chocolat with no sugar added
  12. FRUITS AND VEGETABLES ALL OF THEM OF COURSE
{% elif option == 'sport' %}

Uhhh tough

A healthy diet for sport and exercise should contain plenty of starchy foods, plenty of fruit and vegetables, some protein foods and some dairy foods. It is also important to stay hydrated.

  • Carbohydrates

    The main role of carbohydrates is to provide energy.

    1. Bread
    2. Breakfast cereals and porridge oats
    3. Pasta, noodles
    4. Rice
    5. Potatoes and other starchy vegetables
    6. Soybeans

  • Protein

    Protein is also important for health and physical activity. The main role of protein in the body is for growth, repair and maintenance of body cells and tissues, such as muscle.

    1. Animal sources
      • Tuna
      • Lean beef
      • Shrimps
      • Eggs
      • Salmon
      • Chicken breast
    2. Plant sources
      1. Soy
      2. Tofu
      3. Quinoa

  • Fat

    Fat is an essential nutrient for the body, but it is also a rich source of energy.

    1. Dark chocolate
    2. Butter, lard, ghee, suet, palm oil
    3. Pastries, cakes, biscuits

  • WATER ALWAYS
