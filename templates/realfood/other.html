It's not only about proteins...

Wholemeal cereals and breads, potatoes, pasta and rice are the best energy providers for your body, so the more active you are the more you need. 3 to 5 servings a day is the recommended amount.

Milk, yogurt and cheese provide calcium needed for healthy bones and teeth. Calcium is important during the teenage growth spurt and also for older adults. 3 servings per day or 5 if aged between 9 and 18.

Eggs, beans and nuts provide protein for growth and tissue repair. They are also the main source of iron for healthy blood. 2 servings a day.

Fats, spread and oils provide essential fats but these are only needed in very small amounts. Use as little as possible.

Food and drinks high in fat, sugar and salt should be avoided as they include saturated fat and promote obesity, which can lead to heart disease, type 2 diabetes and some cancers. It is recommended to not have them at all.
